Blue Whale

About this Awesome Animal

The size of a jumbo jet, the blue whale is the largest mammal ever known to have existed. The species is thought to feed almost exclusively on krill (small, shrimp-like crustaceans). In the summer feeding areas, individuals may consume as much as 40 million krill, weighing 4 tonnes, each day. Blue whales have the deepest voice of any animal, and their vocalizations carry for thousands of miles underwater, allowing them to communicate across vast oceans at frequencies below the range of human hearing. For centuries the blue whale was safe from exploitation because of its sheer size and speed. However, the species was driven to the brink of extinction following the development of modern whaling techniques.

Red List Status: Endangered

Cool features:

  1. Is the largest animal to have ever lived in the planet
  2. Has the deepest voice of any animal and can talk to other whales thousands of miles away
  3. A blue whale's blood vessels are wide enough for a human to swim through

Black Rhinoceros

About this Awesome Animal

Black rhinos and white rhinos are not named for their colours, but for the shape of their lips. The black rhino has a prehensile (or grasping) upper lip, which it uses to draw plant material into its mouth. Yellow-brown or grey in colour, this two-horned rhinoceros has a reputation for being unpredictable and dangerous. As a result it has suffered much more persecution than other rhinos. The rhino has two horns made from keratin (the same protein found in human hair and nails). The longest of these horns can grow to over 50 cm in length. Illegal poaching of rhinos for their horns, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine, is one of the main threats to the species.

Red List Status: Critically Endangered

Cool features:

  1. Has a horn to defend itself (made from the same protein found in human hair)
  2. Has thick overlapping skin for protection
  3. Can live up to 50 years old!

Asian Elephant

About this Awesome Animal

The largest land mammal in Asia, this intelligent, highly social animal lives in small groups led by the dominant female, or 'matriarch'. Elephants are large, stocky animals with a strong, flexible trunk, which is an extension of the nose and upper lip! The elephant play a crucial role in its forest ecosystem. Commonly referred to as a ‘keystone' species, it helps to open up forest clearings and distributes the seeds of trees and shrubs. Asian elephants are very social animals. Asian elephants live in family groups consisting of related females and their offspring that are led by the oldest female, the ‘matriarch'. Threatened by poaching and the destruction of the forests in which they live, these magnificent animals are increasingly coming into conflict with the people sharing their habitat.

Red List Status: Endangered

Cool features:

  1. Has a flexible truck to both drink water through and collect food
  2. The tusks, which are used for defence, are actually modified teeth!
  3. Can disperse the seeds of plants by eating them and then passing them out in their dung
